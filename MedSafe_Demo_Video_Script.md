# MedSafe — Demo Video Voiceover Script

**Target length:** ~2 minutes. **Pace:** speak calmly, ~150 words/min. Pause between sections.
Format: **[ON SCREEN]** = what to show · **VO** = what to say.

---

### 0:00–0:12 — Intro (your face)

**[ON SCREEN]** You, on camera. Smile.

**VO:**
"Hi, I'm Ted. This is MedSafe — a tool that helps everyday people catch dangerous drug interactions before they catch them. Let me show you why I built it."

---

### 0:12–0:32 — The problem (photo of medicine bottles, or a simple slide)

**[ON SCREEN]** A photo of several medicine bottles / a pile of pills. Or one plain slide: "Is it safe to take all of this together?"

**VO:**
"On a trip with my family, I watched my father take a handful of medicines — for his liver, for his cholesterol. And I wondered: is it actually safe to take all of these together? He didn't know either. He was just trusting the doctor, without knowing what any of them do. Millions of people are in that exact position."

---

### 0:32–0:45 — The idea (app homepage)

**[ON SCREEN]** MedSafe homepage loading. Cursor hovers over the input.

**VO:**
"So I built MedSafe. You enter your medications — or just take a photo of the label — and it checks them against a real clinical interaction database, in plain language."

---

### 0:45–1:15 — Live demo: typing + results (screen recording)

**[ON SCREEN]** Click "try an example" (or type warfarin, aspirin, ibuprofen). Click Check. Results appear — scroll slowly through the red Major cards and the whole-regimen summary.

**VO:**
"Let's try three common medicines. In one click, MedSafe checks every possible pair — and here's the result: three major interactions, each explained simply. 'Warfarin and aspirin both thin your blood.' No jargon. Above that, an AI-written summary looks at the whole combination together — not just one pair at a time. And it never invents anything: every warning comes from a trusted database."

---

### 1:15–1:40 — Live demo: photo scan (record this on your PHONE)

**[ON SCREEN]** On your phone: tap "Take a photo," camera opens, capture a real medicine label. The drug name appears as a chip to confirm. Tap Check.

**VO:**
"But most people won't type chemical names — so you can just photograph the label. The AI reads the medicine name, you confirm it, and it checks it instantly. This is the part I'm most proud of — it turns a pile of confusing bottles into a clear answer in seconds."

---

### 1:40–1:55 — Schedule + PDF (screen recording)

**[ON SCREEN]** Scroll to the daily schedule. Click "Export PDF for my doctor" — the PDF opens in a new tab.

**VO:**
"It also builds a simple daily schedule, and exports a one-page summary you can hand to your doctor — turning a worried patient into an informed one."

---

### 1:55–2:15 — Close (your face, or title card with the URL)

**[ON SCREEN]** Back to your face, or a title card: "MedSafe · medsafe-innovation26.vercel.app · NextGen Innovation 2026."

**VO:**
"MedSafe is an educational tool — it always points people back to their doctor. But if it helps even one family understand what's in their medicine cabinet, it was worth building. Thanks for watching."

---

## Tips
- **Record voiceover separately** from clicking if talking-while-navigating is stressful — then line up the clips to the words.
- Keep clicks slow and deliberate on screen; judges need a beat to read.
- Show the **phone camera scan** clearly — it's your strongest 15 seconds.
- End on the live URL so judges can try it themselves.
- If you go over 2:15, cut from the problem section (0:12–0:32), not the demo.
